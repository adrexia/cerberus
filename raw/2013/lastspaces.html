Last Spaces Deal - 6 Games for $25

A Special Kind of Lucky Dip Pass

We are now down to our last 20 or so spaces at Chimera 2013 and we would love to fill them! Because there are so few choices of game left, we are reducing our weekend price to the lucky dip price as one-off special. You can choose from any of our remaining games and it will only cost $25 for the whole weekend. With weekend catering only costing $30, that's a weekend of catered gaming for only $55.

Games Available

Friday Night

Fall of the Jaguar Gods

The highly popular flagship game from Hydra 2012, this game is set during the Mayan apocalpyse. Costuming is not at all daunting, a good Mayan costume can be put together easily from a poncho, accentuated with touches of leopard ('jaguar') print, a feathered head dress available from Look Sharp stores and some facepaint. Fall of the Jaguar Gods is a highly commended game, with intense interpersonal relationships, politics, and the end of the world.

Raids at Dusk

A low role-playing 'Capture the Flag' style combat romp across the vast expanse of Motu Moana Scout Camp. No costume necessary, a tabard can be provided.

Saturday Morning

KiL Quest 3

The brave adventurers have been recruited, now KiL Quest 3 seeks monsters to give them a run for their money. A classic fantasy adventure game seeking crew to flesh out a world for daring adventurers. A mix of combat and roleplaying, a high adrenaline and high fun adventure. Costume and weapons provided!

The Captain's Table

Two spaces are available in this previously sold-out drama about the lives of those that carry the pressure of command. The costuming requirements for this game will vary depending on character casting.

Saturday Afternoon

Tournament of Champions

Try your hand at a medieval style round-robin tournament. No experience necessary, costume and weapon can be provided. This is not a roleplaying event, it is a low-key tournament-style combat event run by a veteran of many modern-day medieval battles.

New Voices in Art

An experimental larp exploring the themes of art and ambition. Play an artist 'version' of yourself at an avant-garde art exhibition. Modern day street clothes for costume.

Saturday Night

The Rose and the Dragon

Our epic 160 player flagship event, set in medieval England, incorporating a noble's hall, a village hall, a military encampment and a forest of outlaws. Costuming is basic medieval, and a guide can be found here (2MB, pdf)

Sunday Morning

Party Down at the Glass House

A larp inspired by reality TV shows such as Big Brother, drama and hilarity are bound to ensue! Costume requirements are very low, modern clothes as per what contestants would wear on a reality TV show.

Steampunk Alive! The Last Express

Set in an alternate Victorian history, this game offers intrigue and adventure. The game requires basic steampunk costuming.

The Zone

A post-apocalyptic adventure. Costuming can be put together out of modern day clothes, with an emphasis on survival gear - break out the torn jeans, camo, cargo pants, leather jackets and boots!

Sunday Afternoon

As We Waltzed

Decide the fate of the universe through intricate roleplaying in an elegant ballroom far above the world. Spaces only left for gentleman who are unafraid of a basic waltz, and costuming can be as simple as a suit with a lacy cravat and a Look Sharp top hat.

Victoria Junction

This is a horde game which will have 6 cast characters who will remain as the same characters throughout the game and then a horde group who will play roles for as little as 30 seconds up to an hour or so, as passengers, hotel guests, and assorted others, who give the railway and hotel staff a few problems to deal with! Costuming can be provided for the 'horde' but bringing along your own Victorian bits and pieces is helpful.

Blood and Iron: Teutonic

This is a medieval combat larp, with conflicting sides, open diplomacy and hidden agendas. Costuming is various forms of medieval, depending on the cultural faction you are placed with, your GMs will be able to provide guidelines and support.

How to Sign Up

To sign up for Chimera, please follow this link and fill in our simple registration form. If you select games that have spaces (that is, not wait-listed games) you will automatically be charged the special price of $25 for the weekend. You don't need to play a full six rounds if you don't feel like it, but we won't be able to give you a further discount on top of the $25 - think of it as an all-you-can-larp for $25 deal!

If you have any questions, please feel free to email us on chimera@nzlarps.org

We hope to see you at Chimera 2013!
